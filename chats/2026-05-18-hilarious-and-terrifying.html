Response

You're not making it up, but English doesn't quite have a clean single word for it. A few close candidates, none perfect:

  • Grotesque — in its literary sense (especially Bakhtin's grotesque) this is exactly the comedy-horror fusion you're describing. In everyday usage it's drifted toward just "disgusting," but the original concept covers the laughable-and-unsettling overlap.
  • Macabre — dark and death-tinged, often with a dry comic edge, but it doesn't strictly require humor.
  • Uncanny — unsettling-strange, sometimes funny in effect, but the humor isn't built in.
  • Gallows humor — names the humor itself, not the quality of a thing being both.

If you're remembering a more precise word, my guess is grotesque is what's nagging at you. If not, you might be reaching for the German unheimlich or just the genre label horror-comedy / tragicomic, neither of which is a single descriptor for a thing being simultaneously hilarious and terrifying.
